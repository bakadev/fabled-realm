# Card Symbols

**Attack** — Deal damage equal to the number of Attack symbols to any target player or their Guard card. You may freely choose any opponent to attack.

**Guard** — Play this card in front of you. Each Guard symbol absorbs one point of incoming damage. Place one Wound Token on the card for each point of damage it absorbs. When Wound Tokens equal the number of Guard symbols, the Guard card is destroyed and moved to your Discard Pile. Any excess damage carries over to your Vitality or your next Guard card.

**Heal** — Recover one Vitality per symbol. Vitality cannot exceed your character's starting maximum.

**Draw** — Draw one card per symbol.

**Chain** — Play one additional card from your hand per symbol. Resolving Chain is mandatory — you must play a card for each symbol triggered this turn. If you run out of cards mid-chain, immediately draw two cards and continue.

**Mana** — Gain one Mana token per symbol. Mana tokens are kept in front of you and carry over between turns indefinitely. Spend Mana to activate special abilities or Mana-gated card effects.

---

## Mana

Mana is a deliberate resource, not an automatic one. The Mana symbol most commonly appears alongside Draw and Chain cards, but can appear on any card type where it fits the character's archetype — a healer might generate Mana through ritual healing, a guardian through defensive stances. The tradeoff is strategic: cards that generate Mana tend toward passive or support actions, while pure Attack and Guard cards keep you in the fight.

Mana tokens persist between turns with no maximum. Spend them to Channel a card's enhanced effect or activate Fabled Gifts when the time is right.

### Channel

Channel is the optional empowerment mechanic tied to Mana. It is not a card symbol — it is a secondary effect line printed on eligible cards indicating what spending Mana unlocks.

**How it works:**
- A card lists its base effect via icons as normal
- Below the icons, an optional Channel line shows the Mana cost and enhanced outcome
- Example: a card with two Attack icons might read `Channel 🔵1: +1 ⚔` — paying 1 Mana adds one additional Attack
- If you cannot or choose not to pay the Channel cost, the base effect resolves as normal

**Balance note:** Channel currently grants a flat bonus (+1 of the relevant symbol). Doubling the card's total effect is worth playtesting as an alternative.

## Fabled Gifts

Each character has unique abilities printed on certain cards that reflect their legendary nature. These Fabled Gifts set each character apart and give them their distinct playstyle.

Every Fabled Gift has three tiers determined by how much Mana you spend when you play it:

| Mana Spent | Tier | Effect |
|------------|------|--------|
| 0 | Nat 1 | Dud or negative effect — you may lose a card, lose a Guard, or take damage. Playing a Fabled Gift with no Mana is a risk. |
| 1 | Standard | A useful, reliable effect. The intended baseline for the ability. |
| 2 | Nat 20 | An overpowered effect. High reward for players who have managed their Mana well. |

This mirrors the D&D natural 1 / natural 20 dynamic — you can always play a Fabled Gift, but without Mana you're rolling the dice on something going wrong. Maximum Mana spend per gift is 2.

**Standard deck composition (30 cards total):**

| | Cards |
|-|-------|
| Base cards | 26 |
| Fabled Gift cards (2 types × 2 copies) | 4 |
| **Total** | **30** |

- 6–8 base cards will have a Channel option (across Attack, Guard, Draw, and Chain cards)
- Mana symbol appears on Draw and Chain cards primarily — not on Attack or Guard
- Specific Channel effects per card are determined per character
