# Dracula

## Legend

Dracula has been Dracula for six hundred years and considers this sufficient qualification for anything.

He did not write the mythology. That was a group effort, assembled over several centuries by bards, clerics, traumatized scholars, and at least one cartographer who had never been within four hundred miles of Transylvania and was mostly guessing. The coffin was a medical situation. The accent is an exaggeration. The dramatic swooping was a practical transportation solution that has since been reframed in ways he finds reductive. He has been attempting to correct the record since approximately 1380. The record has different representation and is not interested in a meeting. In the meantime, he maintains. The laugh has been through seventeen drafts. The entrance at the Realm's first major tournament took eleven minutes. He was told this was a lot. He took it as a compliment.

What he is, definitively, is the original. The template. The reason there is a thriving professional industry whose job title begins with "vampire" and ends with something he finds inconvenient at dinner. He acknowledges this is good for the brand. He wishes they would call ahead.

He arrived in the Realm with documentation. Several trunks of it — correspondence, title deeds, receipts from feuds dating back to 1340 that he considers very much ongoing. His information on regional politics is six centuries deep and aggressively confident. Some of it is still accurate. He doesn't know which parts.

The castle is understaffed. This has been the case for decades. He has had many thralls over the centuries and they have, without exception, left. They wander off, pledge themselves to different dark lords, or quit via note. He offered eternal servitude and a formal title. He considered this a competitive package. No one has explained to him why it wasn't.

He is the most magnetic person in every room he enters. He is aware of this because it is always true and has always been true and he assumes this is just how rooms work. He has never once correctly identified when someone was afraid of him versus drawn to him. He considers these the same thing. He is, on this specific point, very wrong.

He will not discuss the garlic thing.

## Playstyle
Low Vitality, high mobility. Cycles aggressively through his deck to find the right card at the right moment. Generates Mana through offense and Draw rather than defense. Minimal Guard — he's not built to absorb punishment. Chain combos build theatrical momentum. Fragile but dangerous if left alone to build resources.

**Design notes:**
- 14 Draw vs Frankenstein's 0 is the defining contrast — Dracula hunts for the right moment, Frank grinds through whatever he has.
- Mana lives on Attack and Draw cards only — never on Guard. He powers up through aggression and information, not endurance.
- 6 Guard and 10 Vitality means he can be killed fast. That's the trade-off. He needs to hit his Mana and use his gifts before he runs out of room.
- Channel effects TBD — likely on Big Attack and Draw cards.

### Character Design

**Scale:** Tall, filling the frame vertically through presence rather than mass. He doesn't take up space the way Frankenstein does — he occupies it deliberately. There is a difference and he is aware of it.

**Build:** Lean, angular, impeccably postured. Six centuries of practice. He has been working on this silhouette for a long time and it shows.

**Skin:** Very pale, almost grey. Sharp features. Nothing grotesque — he looks like someone who has been indoors for six hundred years and is fine with it.

**Expression:** The practiced version of menacing. Slightly too studied, slightly too composed. You can tell he worked on this. The smile is present. The fangs are not visible. That is intentional on his part.

**Clothing:** He has been wealthy for six centuries and it shows in the way that becomes a problem. Multiple eras of peak fashion, worn simultaneously, with complete confidence. Everything is slightly overdone. He does not see this.

**Eyes:** Unsettling in a "you cannot quite look away" direction — not horror-red, not glowing. More like he's already looking at where you're going to look next.

### The Statement Cloak

Enormous. Dramatic. Doing a great deal of work. It predates the castle and has been a statement since 1401. The color is deep red-black, which he selected deliberately, and it sweeps in ways that suggest either very good tailoring or minor eldritch influence. Possibly both.

It is the first thing anyone notices. He is aware of this. He planned it.

### Held Items

**One hand:** Raised in a welcoming gesture — mid-entrance, theatrical, practiced. It doubles as an eldritch casting pose. He does not distinguish between these uses.

**Other hand:** A wine glass. Contents ambiguous — deep red, could be wine, could be something else, the art should not clarify this. He has been holding it for a while. He was expecting company.

**No weapon.** He is the Warlock. His presence is the weapon. He would agree with this assessment enthusiastically and at length.

### Color Palette

- Deep red and black for the cloak — classic, deliberate, exactly what you'd expect, which is exactly what he wants
- Near-grey skin
- Gold accents throughout — rings, clasps, trim — six centuries of accumulated wealth
- Warm candlelight or torchlight suggested in the background, not daylight under any circumstances

### Tone

He should look like he knows you're looking at him. He has positioned himself to be looked at. The pose, the cloak, the expression — it is all a performance and it is all on purpose. The art should capture the performance, not the performer behind it.

**What to avoid:**
- Nosferatu grotesque — wrong Dracula entirely
- Genuinely menacing expression — he performs menace, he does not have it
- Romantic brooding — that's a different franchise, leave it there
- Obvious fangs front and center — keep them implied
- Modern vampire aesthetic (leather, underworld, etc.) — he is period-specific, just several periods at once
- Anything that makes him look sad or lonely — he is not suffering, he is networking
