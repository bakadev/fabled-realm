# Frankenstein

## Legend

No one knows where Dr. Frankenstein went. (No one asked. There was a fire. There is always a fire.)

What showed up in the Realm six weeks later was big, green, smelled faintly of ozone and regret, and was asking directions to the nearest inn. Politely. He asked twice because he didn't understand the first answer. He has never understood a first answer.

His name is not Frankenstein. Frankenstein was the doctor. He will explain this if you ask. He will explain it if you don't ask. He has explained it thirty-seven times this week. This has not stopped anyone from calling him Frankenstein, and it has not stopped him from turning around when they do, which he considers a personal failing.

He is not evil. He's just catastrophic. There's a difference, and it's mostly paperwork.

His Constitution is 20. He has survived a house fire, a trebuchet, two separate lightning storms (one intentional), and an encounter with a deity that the temple in question has since reclassified as "a weather event." His Intelligence is 6. He remembers none of this. He's mostly fine.

The neck bolts do something. Possibly several things. One wizard spent three weeks studying them, wrote a six-page report, and immediately set it on fire. The bolts crackle when it rains. He thinks it's nice.

He became an adventurer because someone handed him a pamphlet and it had a picture of a sword on it and he liked that. He has been extremely good at adventuring in the way that a landslide is good at redecorating.

## Playstyle
High Vitality, absorbs damage, slow and unstoppable. Strong Guard cards, few big hits, some regeneration, very few Chain cards. Mana generated through defensive and healing actions rather than aggression.

**Design notes:**
- No Draw cards — Frankenstein doesn't cycle. He plays what he has and grinds through it.
- Mana lives on Guard and Heal cards only — never on Attack. He doesn't generate power from aggression.
- Channel effects TBD — likely on Big Attack and Heavy Guard cards.
