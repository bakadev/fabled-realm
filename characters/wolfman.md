# Wolfman

## Legend

The Wolfman is the greatest tracker in the Realm. This is not a reputation. It is a documented fact, across multiple independent sources, including three hunter's guilds, two royal cartographers, and one very thorough incident report from the city of Vastenholm that concludes, on page seven, that no other explanation is possible.

He can follow a three-day-old scent through rain. He can identify who passed through an area, how many there were, what direction they came from, and what one of them had for breakfast. He can track for days without stopping. He has done this across four countries, two mountain ranges, and one extremely confusing forest that professional pathfinders avoid on principle. Nobody asked him to do it for that long. He finished anyway.

He always comes back.

The nose is the problem and the solution and these are not separate issues. The same instrument that makes him the best tracker in recorded history is receiving approximately ten thousand inputs at all times. Most of them he manages. Occasionally one of them

He's back. The point is: he's back. He picks up exactly where he left off. Nothing important happened while he was gone. He has verified this to his own satisfaction and is ready to continue.

When he gets the right scent — not one of the ten thousand, but *the* one — something changes. The distractions stop. Whatever else was happening ceases to matter because this is the one and he is going to follow it to the end of everything and possibly further. He has tracked things that were considered untraceable. He has come back from all of them, successful, on time by his reckoning, ready to tell you what he found.

He has no complicated feelings about being a wolf. He is simply one.

## Playstyle
Medium Vitality, medium Guard, high Chain. Generates Mana through momentum — Chain cards are his power engine, not defense or aggression. More Chain cards than any other character; rewards consecutive sequential plays. Self-sustains with moderate Heal. Not a tank, not a glass cannon — relentless. The longer the fight goes, the more dangerous he becomes.

### Character Design

**Scale:** Lean and functional. Not the biggest character — that's Frankenstein's role. He fills the frame through posture and readiness rather than mass. He looks like he could be gone before you finish looking at him.

**Build:** Tireless. CON 14 means he's built for the long chase, not the heavy hit. Wiry, efficient, no wasted material. Everything about him is optimized for moving a long time.

**Fur and features:** Natural, not styled. Wolf features — muzzle, ears, claws — that look functional rather than monstrous. The ears are up. They are always up. The nose is the most important feature on his face and should look like it's working even when nothing else is happening.

**Expression:** Present. Focused. Not intense — focused is different. He's tracking something. His eyes might be looking in a slightly different direction from where his body is pointing. The tension between locked-in and distracted should both be visible simultaneously.

**Posture:** Weight forward. Always almost moving. Even in a static illustration he should look like he's mid-stride or about to be. He is not standing — he is pausing, briefly, between movements.

### Clothing and Gear

Ranger practical — worn, weathered, built for movement. Earth tones throughout: brown, grey, forest green, tan. Nothing shiny. Nothing decorative. That is Dracula's territory and he has no interest in it.

No heavy armor — he doesn't absorb hits, he avoids them. Light layering that doesn't restrict movement. A weathered cloak or jacket, worn in. Pouches, a satchel — a tracker carries things. The gear looks used because it has been used, extensively, often longer than anyone asked him to use it.

### Weapon

His claws are his primary weapon. A hunting knife as a secondary — practical, worn handle, not decorative. No bow — his cards are "Right Behind" and "End Of The Trail." He runs them down. Distance is not his method.

### Color Palette

- Earth tones throughout — brown, grey, forest green, muted tan
- Fur natural and unstyled — no grooming, this is a working animal
- No bright colors, no metallic shine
- Lighting should suggest outdoors, overcast — tracking weather, not dramatic torch-glow

### Tone

He should look like a professional doing his job. Not a monster. Not a tragic figure. Not a dramatic one. A very good tracker who is currently tracking something, and possibly also something else that just caught his attention.

**What to avoid:**
- Mid-transformation or any Jekyll/Hyde implication — he is always this, this is not a state
- Howling at the moon — too obvious, too cliché, also he is busy
- Tortured or pained expression — he has no complicated feelings about any of this
- Overly feral or monstrous — he's a Ranger, he's functional, he has a job
- Heavy armor or a shield — he doesn't block, he moves
- Styled or groomed fur — he has been in four countries and two mountain ranges, it shows
